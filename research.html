Research

My current work is in numerical analysis and computer simulations. The focus is on the design, development, and analysis, of new finite element methods for scientific computing.

Gravitational Wave Telescopes

To detect gravitational waves, several new observatories are being built. Computer simulations are essential for interpreting the data. This work is on the design and implementation of a new computer simulation for the propagation of gravitational waves. By recognizing the nature of each wave, these observatories can be turned into new telescopes that see gravity, providing entirely new windows into the Universe. Acoustic Wave Propagation

Applications of wave propagation are numerous, ranging from acoustic waves for radar and elastic waves for earthquake detection, to electromagnetic waves for cellphone design. This work provided the first a priori error analysis of a new family of discontinuous finite element methods for the acoustic equation. Image: Acoustic scattering from an airfoil.
